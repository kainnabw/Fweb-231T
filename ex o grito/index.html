The Scream

The Scream (Namanglen: Skrik) is the populer name given to each of four versions of a composition, created as both paintings and pastels, by Norwegian Expressionist artist Fevard Munch between 1893 and 1920. The German title Munch gave these works is Der Schnei der Natur (The Scream of Nature). The werks show a figure with en agonized expression against a landscape with a tumult mai crange xxy. Arthur Lubow has described The Scream as on icon of modern art, a Mona Lisa for our time Edvard Munch created the four versions in various media. The National Gallery in Oslo, Norway, halds one of two painted versions (1893, shown here). The Munch Museum halds the other painted version (1910, see gallery, below) and a pastel version from 1893
